TURBINE MODEL

The turbine model captures wind energy from air conditioner outdoor units, efficiently converting it into electrical power through specialized generators. In off-grid scenarios, DC power (24V) is stored and converted to versatile AC (12V/220V). For on-grid applications, the turbine integrates with centralized AC units, generating up to 1150 watts. The blade design, based on the NACA 4412 airfoil, undergoes rigorous testing for optimal efficiency. Real-time monitoring, facilitated by AI, ensures oversight of turbine performance, motor health, and weather conditions through a connected mobile application. The model's benefits include contributing to renewable energy, adaptability to diverse scenarios, and potential seamless integration with existing infrastructure. Challenges involve addressing wind variability and integration complexities. Continuous optimization and monitoring tech advancements are deemed crucial for long-term viability in the evolving renewable energy landscape.
